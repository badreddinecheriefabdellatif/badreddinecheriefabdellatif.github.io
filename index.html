About me

I will start (soon) a postdoctoral research position in the Department of Statistics at the University of Oxford, working with Arnaud Doucet.

Prior to that, I received a PhD in statistics at CREST (Center for Research in Economics and Statistics), Paris, under the supervision of Pierre Alquier, currently research scientist at RIKEN AIP in Tokyo. My main research interests lie in variational inference for approximating intractable posterior distributions in Bayesian statistics and machine learning. More precisely, my work focuses on the statistical properties of variational approximations and the design and understanding of efficient algorithms for computing them in an online fashion. I'm also interested in robust statistics, and I investigate Maximum Mean Discrepancy based estimators as learning rules that are robust to model misspecification.

You can find here my PhD thesis.
